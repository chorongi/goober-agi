## **Scene 2: The MM Goober Connection**
**Visual:** `assets/video_slides/overview.png`  
**Duration:** 0:20 - 0:50  
**Voiceover:**  
"Today’s AI is brilliant at retrieving facts but remains fundamentally blind to the real-time chaos and nuance of human social environments. This is why I built **MM Goober**: a zero-annotation benchmark that leverages the rich, infinite data of 24/7 YouTube Live streams to rigorously test Social Theory of Mind. By processing live video frames and scrolling chat synchronized in real-time, we demand the AI construct a coherent 'world model' of the stream. This approach completely eliminates the need for human annotators, creating a scalable test that cannot be gamed by simple memorization."

## **Scene 3: Task 1 - Social Foresight**
**Visual:** `assets/video_slides/task1.png`  
**Duration:** 0:50 - 1:15  
**Voiceover:**  
"Task 1 evaluates Social Foresight, testing the AI's predictive capabilities in a live setting. The AGI observes 30 seconds of video and chat history, and must predict the exact sequence of messages that will appear in the subsequent 10-second window. We utilize an advanced LLM-as-a-Judge through the Kaggle SDK to measure semantic similarity, reaction flow, and underlying sentiment. This essentially serves as a dynamic test of Theory of Mind, proving whether the AI truly understands the emotional momentum of the crowd."

---

## **Scene 4: Task 2 - Cognitive Reconstruction**
**Visual:** `assets/video_slides/task2.png`  
**Duration:** 1:15 - 1:40  
**Voiceover:**  
"Task 2 pushes the boundaries with Cognitive Reconstruction, testing the AI's ability to infer hidden states. After observing a stream, we completely withhold the video feed for 10 seconds, forcing the AI to 'see' the world solely through the incoming chat logs. By demanding the AI generate accurate text descriptions of the missing visual frames based purely on social reactions, it demonstrates it has successfully mapped human language to complex physical and situational world states. It proves the AI can correlate a sudden 'F in the chat' to a specific on-screen failure."

## **Scene 5: Task 3 - Context Agility**
**Visual:** `assets/video_slides/task3.png`  
**Duration:** 1:40 - 2:05  
**Voiceover:**  
"Finally, Task 3 measures Context Agility, addressing a critical flaw in current large models: contextual inertia. We subject the AGI to a 'Hard Switch'—instantly moving it from a gaming stream to a completely different context, like a live news feed, giving it only a 5-second glance to orient itself. We then measure its Zero-Shot Adaptation by asking it to immediately predict the new chat environment. A truly general intelligence must be capable of discarding outdated assumptions and instantly recalibrating its social world model to a new reality."
